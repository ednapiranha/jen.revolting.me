Interfaces of the Future

Think about a massive multi-developer virtual space that enables multiple developers to work on the same software in realtime and in three dimensions. How would we design such a system? How would it change developer ergonomics and general design and usability decisions? Or how we could communicate with others beyond text messages, photos, emoji and videos. What if we used physical actions in an augmented environment to act out what we want to say?

Developing Software

For software development, we currently type into a two dimensional space to create our output. For two dimensions, it is easy to use an X and Y grid to map out everything. When a third dimension is added, it becomes less intuitive to imagine where Z is and the process to operate in that world is awkward and unnatural. I am using ThreeJS a lot these days and still find it difficult to place things in the right position. When I change the camera's position, I don't intuitively remember the coordinates for every object in an efficient way.

As a result, I started thinking about manipulating the real environment and interacting with our physical space to easily control where the coordinates are. If we could step into a virtual reality environment to move objects around in order to create code, it would open opportunities for how we understand what a programming language is and what it means to code.

No need for keyboards or cameras?

When we socialize with others online, we send text messages, emoji, videos and photos. All of our existing iterations involve slightly different ways of presenting our limited media, such as filters, sizes and colors to name a few.

If we take this into a three dimensional environment, we can send someone a physical movement as a form of communication. We can even send the movement to multiple recipients at the same time. Body movement, virtual environment setting and mood can all change how we socialize to go beyond our standard set of tools. We wouldn't need keyboards or cameras for every situation - or at all.

Combining Two Worlds?

Merging software development and online communications through bodily movement could create a new kind of IDE. With the concerns of sedentary lifestyles and our increased dependency in technology and software, we have the opportunity to rethink what it means to be engineers.
